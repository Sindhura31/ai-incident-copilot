STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE - EMAIL SERVICE FAILURE

Incident Type: Email Service Failure
Severity: High
Owner: Communication Infrastructure Team

SYMPTOMS:
- Users unable to send or receive emails
- SMTP connection refused errors
- Email queue backing up
- Bounce messages increasing

INVESTIGATION STEPS:
1. Check email server health and availability
2. Verify SMTP service is running on correct port
3. Review email server logs for error patterns
4. Check DNS MX records are correctly configured
5. Verify firewall rules allow SMTP traffic (port 25, 465, 587)
6. Check disk space on email server
7. Verify SSL certificates for email service are valid

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
- SMTP relay service crashed or stopped
- Firewall rule change blocking SMTP ports
- Disk space full preventing new emails
- SSL certificate expiry causing connection failures
- DNS misconfiguration affecting mail routing

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
1. Restart SMTP relay service immediately
2. Update firewall rules to allow SMTP traffic
3. Free up disk space if storage is full
4. Renew SSL certificates if expired
5. Correct DNS MX records if misconfigured

ESCALATION:
- L2: Email Infrastructure Team
- L3: Cloud Infrastructure Team
- Vendor: Email service provider support

VERIFICATION:
- Send test email and confirm delivery
- Check email queue is draining normally
- Monitor bounce rate for 30 minutes
- Confirm users can send and receive emails
- Close incident after normal mail flow confirmed
